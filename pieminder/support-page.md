# Support — PieMinder Pro

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why are my ingredient amounts different from another recipe I've used?**
PieMinder Pro uses baker's percentages calibrated for a consistent, home-baker-friendly dough. Every baker has a slightly different formula — the key is to find settings that work for your oven, your flour brand, and your taste, then save them.

**What does "hydration" mean?**
Hydration is the ratio of water to flour, expressed as a percentage. A 65% hydration dough (the default) is easy to work with and produces a classic chewy crust. Higher hydration (70–80%) creates a more open, airy crumb but is stickier and harder to shape. Lower hydration (60%) gives a crispier, denser result.

**Why does the app recommend 65% hydration?**
65% is a reliable starting point for most home ovens and all-purpose or bread flour. Once you're comfortable with the dough, try 68–72% for a more Neapolitan-style result.

**My dough is too sticky / too dry. What should I adjust?**
- Too sticky → lower the hydration by 2–3%
- Too dry → raise the hydration by 2–3%
Small adjustments make a big difference. The slider lets you fine-tune in 1% increments.

**What's the gluten-free mode?**
Gluten-free mode uses a separate flour formula and defaults to 75% hydration, which GF flour blends typically need to achieve a similar texture. You can adjust the hydration for your specific GF flour blend.

**Can I share my recipe with someone?**
Yes — tap "Share This Recipe" on the main screen. The link encodes all your current settings. When the recipient opens it, the calculator is already set to your exact recipe.

**The tip jar says I'm offline.**
The tip jar requires an internet connection to process payment through Apple. Your calculator settings and recipe still work fully offline.

**I made a tip purchase but nothing happened.**
If a purchase was charged, Apple will have a receipt in your purchase history (Settings → [Your Name] → Media & Purchases). The tip is a one-time consumable — there's nothing to unlock in the app. If you believe there was an error, contact Apple Support at reportaproblem.apple.com.
